Internet Explorer Security

This page lists some of the security settings you can use in Internet Explorer. The Safe uses the settings from Explorer when accessing your Digital ID.

Internet Explorer has three basic security settings.

  • Low
    • means Internet Explorer stores the password for your private key. The Safe can use your Digital IDs without you having to provide the password for the private key. You will still have to enter the password for the Safe.
  • Medium
    • means the Safe will prompt you once for your private key password when first start the Safe.
  • High
    • means you will be prompted each time the Safe uses your Digital ID. This means that each time you encrypt or decrypt a file, the Safe will ask you to provide your private key password. Depending on the settings you use in the Safe, you might also have to provide the Safe password.
