Installing a Digital ID

There are four steps to getting and installing a Digital ID:

  • Enroll through the Digital ID Center.
  • Check your e-mail for a response from the Center.
  • Pick up your Digital ID from the Center.
  • Install the Digital ID in Microsoft Internet Explorer.
Enrolling
When enrolling for a Digital ID, Microsoft Internet Explorer (IE) lets you assign additional security to your private key. If you select the added protection, you will be asked to select High, Medium or Low security before completing your Digital ID enrollment.

These security settings are provided to help protect your private key, which resides in your computer's registry. Your private key is the part of your Digital ID that only you are meant to have. Every time you use your Digital ID your private key is accessed. The Medium and High options assure that your private key is only being accessed with your permission.

High requires you to enter a password before your private key is accessed by IE.

Medium alerts you and ask for your permission before your private key is accessed. You don't have to enter your password, but you do have to confirm the use of your Digital ID.

Low does not add any additional security. This means your private key is protected only by your system's log-in password.

If you select the High security option, you will be asked to assign a password to your browser. Only you know this password, so select something memorable and secure. If you cannot recall your password, you will need to revoke your current Digital ID, and replace your Digital ID in the Digital ID Center.

The final enrollment window asks for your password and generates the keys to be used in your Digital ID.

E-mail and retrieval
After you enroll for a Digital ID, you receive an e-mail message from the Digital ID Center that contains a PIN and a URL where you can pick up your Digital ID. To pick up the Digital ID:

  1. Copy the Digital ID PIN and then click on the URL provided in the same e-mail message.
  2. Paste your Digital ID PIN into the field on the form. Click the Submit button and follow instructions to complete your Digital ID installation and backup.

Installation
The retrieval form contains an Install button that starts the installation process. Follow the onscreen directions. It's a good idea back up your new Digital ID to a floppy disk and store it in a safe place after the installation process.

The congratulations page lets you know you've completed installation successfully. If you want to use your Digital ID in another application, you can export it from Internet Explorer. If you use Outlook or Outlook Express for e-mail, you do not have to export and import the Digital ID because Outlook will automatically use the Digital IDs installed in Explorer. Also, if you have another Digital ID that you want to use in Internet Explorer, you can import the ID. See the section on importing and exporting for complete details.

Before you can send signed mail, you must associate your Digital ID with the e-mail account you want to use it with. To associate the Digital ID:

  1. Choose Tools > Accounts from the Outlook menu.
  2. Select the account you want to use your ID with.
  3. Click Properties, and then click the Security tab. Check the box named Use a digital ID when sending secure messages, and then click Digital ID.
  4. Select the Digital ID you want to associate with this account (only the Digital IDs with the same e-mail address as the e-mail address for the account will be shown).
