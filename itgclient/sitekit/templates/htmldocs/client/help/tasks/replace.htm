Replacing a Digital ID

If you lose your Digital ID before backing it up, you can replace it. The new Digital ID will have the same validity period as the original ID.

To replace a Digital ID:

  1. Go to the Digital ID Center and click the Replace button at the top of the page.
  2. Enter the e-mail address, name, or serial number of the Digital ID you want to replace. You do not need to enter all three pieces of information. Searching by e-mail address is the easiest method, because you are most likely to know the exact e-mail address registered with your ID. E-mail addresses must be entered in ASCII characters only
    The information you enter must exactly match the information in the Digital ID. For example, if you look up a Digital ID by specifying the name Marj Baldwin, only the Digital ID that contains the name Marj Baldwin would be found. Digital IDs under the names Marjorie Baldwin or M. Baldwin would not be returned as matches. If your administrator has set your service for a character set other than ASCII English, be sure that the name you are searching for matches the character set you are using.
  3. Select the type of Digital ID you want to replace. If you are not sure whether the Digital ID you are looking for is valid, revoked, expired, or pending, select "All" for a comprehensive search, and then click the Search button.
  4. You see a list of Digital IDs to choose from. Click the link for the Digital ID that you want to replace.
  5. The page displays more information about the Digital ID. Make sure this is the correct ID you want to replace, and then click the Replace button.
  6. On the Web page that appears, type your Challenge Phrase and click the "Replace This Certificate" button. The Challenge Phrase is the text you entered when you enrolled for a Digital ID. Without the Challenge Phrase, you cannot manage your Digital IDs.
