Passwords in Firefox

Passwords protect Digital IDs from unauthorized use. You need to remember the following passwords:

  • The Challenge Phrase lets you manage your Digital ID using the Digital ID Center. You create your own Challenge Phrase when you enroll for a Digital ID.
  • Your private key password, stored in the browser, lets you use your Digital ID when proving your identity to Web sites. For example, you need to enter the password before Firefox sends your Digital ID to a Web site.
When first enrolling for a Digital ID, Firefox requires you to assign a password to protect your Digital ID. This password functions as a way for you (and only you) to use your Digital ID.

Your private key is the part of your Digital ID that only you are meant to have. Firefox stores your private key in a file called key3.db. However, when you export or save your Digital ID, your ID and private key are stored in the same file, usually with the extension .p12. Because your private key is required to access any encrypted messages sent to you, you should save your Digital ID and store it in a secure place. You should also back up your data frequently. If your hard disk crashes or you experience some other computer failure, the only way to retrieve the data is if you have a backup copy and a backup of your Digital ID and private key.
