Encrypting a message

If someone intercepts your e-mail messages, it is relatively easy for them to read. The only way you can be sure that your e-mail is private is to scramble the contents by encrypting your messages. This way, only the person you send it to can read it.

To encrypt a message, you need to have a copy of the other person's Digital ID. When you receive a signed message from someone, Netscape Messenger automatically stores the sender's Digital ID for you, so you can easily send them encrypted messages. If you don't have someone's Digital ID, you can either ask them to send you a signed message or you can search for their Digital ID in a directory.

You can encrypt individual messages or configure your security preferences to automatically encrypt all e-mail messages to recipients whose Digital IDs are stored in your address book.
